About Our Village

Introduction & History

Gram Panchayat Mandur was established on 10 December 1979. It includes two revenue villages: Mandur and Patilwadi. Mandur is situated on the scenic banks of the Kumbhi river.

The village is 25 km from Gaganbawada Taluka and 35 km from Kolhapur District. The total area of the village is 546.11 hectares.

Socio-Economic Status

Occupation: 90% of the population is engaged in agriculture. The main crop is Sugarcane, while Rice, Nachani, and Vari are grown during the monsoon.

Cultural Heritage: Major temples include Khandoba Temple (Patilwadi) and Rameshwar Temple (Mandur). Dasara and Mahashivratri are celebrated with great enthusiasm.
